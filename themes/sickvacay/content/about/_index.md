Amy Mackelden is the weekend editor at *[Marie Claire US](https://www.marieclaire.com/author/amy-mackelden/)* and a freelance entertainment journalist, living with multiple sclerosis, ADHD, PCOS, and anxiety (among other things). Her bylines include *[ELLE](https://href.li/?http://www.elle.com/author/17479/amy-mackelden/)*, *[People](https://people.com/author/amy-mackelden/)*, *[Harper’s BAZAAR](https://www.harpersbazaar.com/author/17479/amy-mackelden/)*, *[The Rumpus](https://therumpus.net/2020/07/the-rumpus-interview-with-abi-palmer/)*, *[The Independent](https://href.li/?http://www.independent.co.uk/author/amy-mackelden)*, *[HelloGiggles](https://href.li/?https://hellogiggles.com/author/amy-mackelden/)*, *[Cosmopolitan](https://href.li/?http://www.cosmopolitan.com/author/17479/amy-mackelden/)*, & *[Bustle](https://href.li/?http://www.bustle.com/authors/1333-amy-mackelden)*.

<img src="/images/retail.webp" alt="Retail" class="about__img">

Amy co-edited the *[Emma Press Anthology of Illness](https://theemmapress.com/shop/anthology-of-illness/)*, and has written about health for Forbes, Healthline, *[The Paper Gown](https://thepapergown.zocdoc.com/author/amymackeldengmail-com/)* by Zocdoc, The Checkup by SingleCare, MS Society, MS Trust, and Byrdie. In 2019, she undertook Columbia University's short course in Narrative Medicine funded by a Developing Your Creative Practice grant from Arts Council England.

After winning a Northern Writers’ Award from New Writing North in 2011, Amy co-founded [Butcher’s Dog](https://www.butchersdogmagazine.co.uk/) poetry magazine. She has toured several spoken word theatre projects, funded by Grants for the Arts Awards, and received two Artists'​ International Development Fund Awards from Arts Council England.
